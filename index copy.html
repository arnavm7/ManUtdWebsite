About

Manchester United is one of the most historically dominant football clubs in Europe. In recent times, however, they have struggled to reach similar dominant heights, as their last Premier League title win was in 2013. Current manager, Ole Gunnar Solskjaer, has built a squad of world class players. Many question his ability to coach a top side. Will United succeed? Stay tuned with this page, representing fans' voice.

Previous Managing History

Here is a brief history of the club and it's management.

Founding

Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway

1878-1945

As the First Owners, L&Y Railway founded "Newton Heath Football Club"

  • 3x League Winners, FA Cup, 2x Community Shields
  • Long Term Managers: Ernest Mangnall, Jack Robson, John Chapman

Growth Era

Sir Matt Busby

1945-1969

Responsible for starting the success of the big team that we know today. Won 5 titles, 2 FA Cups, 5 Shields, and 1 European Cup in his reign. His energetic team, often called "The Busby Babes", were a testament to his faith in young players.

Sir Matt Busby accomplished much depsite suffering a great loss in the Munich Air Disaster of 1958, when 8 of the first team players died in a team airplane accident. He went on to rebuild the team with star players along the likes of Sir Bobby Charlton, George Best, and Denis Law.

Sir Alex Ferguson

1986-2013

The Man. The Myth. The Legend. And, a King who would retake the throne almost every season. Football gurus and fans widely agree that Sir Alex and the "Fergie" era showcases the greatest heights a manager could reach. He won 13 Premier League titles, 5 FA Cups, 4 League Cups, 10 Community Shields, and 2 Champions Leagues (among many other trophies) with United.

Sir Alex is the history of United. He is especially known for developing Cristiano Ronaldo (one of the greatest players of all time). His tactics were constantly changing, which could confuse opponents. He also brought the best out of generally mediocre players. One of the most famous moments in his reign was the 1999 European League Final win against Bayern Munich, when the then-striker Ole Gunnar scored the winning goal in extra time. The commentator screamed, "And Solskjaer has won it!"

Post-Fergie Era

David Moyes

2013 - 2014

Known as the manager who ignited United's recent downfall.

In his one season in charge, David Moyes failed to win more than one trophy and finished the league in United's lowest ever Premier League position, 7th/20. However, Manchester United's poor management under the care of the Glazer Family, may have contributed to this harsh reality of life without Fergie.

Louis Van Gaal

2014 - 2016

Won a Community Shield.

Louis Van Gaal had an arsenal of attacking talent with Robin Van Persie, Wayne Rooney, loanee signing Radamel Falcao, and world class attacking midfielder Di Maria. However, his defensive style-of-play provided little to no service for these attacking players. A defensive approach meant more shots on goal, leading to the growth of now legendary United goalkeeper, David De Gea.

Jose Mourinho

2016 - 2018

Won 1 Europa League, 1 League Cup, and 1 Community Shield with United.

Jose Mourinho has an impressive CV of club management, even before arriving at United. However, he is often criticized for bringing in expensive players just because they had a big name. For example, issues in the midfield and central defense were ignored for the signing of star striker Zlatan Ibrahimovic. Furthermore, Jose created a separation between himself and the team, often treating them harshly. Luke Shaw and Paul Pogba, two current key players, have prospered after Jose's departure.

Fans Voice: Manchester United News and Opinion

Every club is strengthened by it's fan base. Hence, the fans' voice is important to highlight, especially when considering that big club decisions are made by those with power. Here are some outstanding Man United fan news and analysis channels, and what they specialize in.

The United Stand

Started by YouTuber Mark Goldbridge, the United Stand has created a community of over 1 million subscribers. Posts day to day live stream (fan-interactive) discussions on United's management, match reactions, live watch-alongs, and interviews with notable insider sources (such as trasfer market analyst Fabrizio Romano).

Statman Dave

Dave posts in depth tactical analyis videos of Man Utd's gameplay. He focuses on the roles of individual players and how their play can lead to more efficient team play.

Stretford Paddock

Similar to The United Stand, Stretford Paddock posts fan related content with watch-alongs and tactical review after games. However, they additionally stream live fan reaction videos, and interview fans outside of the stadium post game.

Stretford End Arising

SEA is an online forum (similar to reddit), where you can create new Manchester United-related discussion posts to get the opinions of thousands of other users.
